Safety KeyChain

In my first year design group, we implemented this safety keychain. This project was designed for upper year university students to use when they face dangerous situations. It has four main functions, a flashlight for better visibility, a sound alarm to alert others, a "send-a-text" feature to selected contacts and a 911 help button. A wireframe of the complimentary app which allows users to set up contacts and customized text message, was also designed by us. This project exercised many components of the design cycle as well as use of SolidWorks, Arduino and Proto.io.

Puzzle Box

As a group, we designed a puzzle box using SolidWorks and 3D-printing. Puzzle boxes are essentially a series of simple puzzle steps that allow the user to open the box. We decided on creating a puzzle box with our program name. So the lid of the box has a track and letters hook on that track in different ways. Essentially, the "S" holds the box closed by sliding in the lid. Next slides in the Y, then D. E hooks on the knob in the corner. The user has to first twist off the E, slide the D vertically, slide the Y horizontally over and vertically out and finally slide the S off to open the box.

My focus was to design six pieces in such a way that the user would find it intuitive but also be challenged. Each group member then went on their own to create their piece. I worked on the "S" which was the final connecting piece between the box. It was challenging because it had connections with other many different pieces. I used SolidWorks to model the system to see its interaction and would make improvements in an iterative manner.

Employee Management System

For this project, my goal was to come up with a system that would make the employee management easier in a small business setting. For software companies, Jira works well for issues, time-tracking and other key activities important for software teams. However, in smaller companies, alot of that work is manual. TimeTracker is a system that will allow employers to have all information about their employees in one place. It does their hour and wage calculations, keeps track of their vacation requests and any other work-related information. It gives employees the ability to log in, track their daily hours and put in virtual vacation requests all in one place, without the hasstle of going on a hunt for an HR representative.

I am using Django and python as well as HTML and CSS for its development. I thought this would work well because Django already provides an admin interface which is efficient and also I felt I could learn a lot more about Django and Python by doing this.

Aut2Learn

This project was created in a hackathon, Equithon 2018 which had a theme of equity. This app was created to accelerate learning for individuals with Autism and provide them with the support they need to be able to reach their maximum potential.

Initially, this project required alot of user research about Autism and treatments that are in current use. ABA or Applied Behavioural Analysis therapy consistently showed up as an effective treatment for Autism. Essentially, this approach focuses on teaching motor, behavioural and everyday skills. It values positive reinforcement and can also be conducted by anyone easily. Thus, this was the approach taken for this app.

The app essentially has two main pathways, one for the student and one for the parent/therapist. However, both accounts are under the login of the guardian. After logging in as the student, several different activities appear on the screen. These range from everyday task activities and situational games. The user engages in the app by picking the category, completing the activity and achieving points which show up in their profile. They also have a scoreboard where they can unlock trophies after certain point accumulations.

In the parents side of the app, their home screen opens to a progress tracker of the student where they can see how many activities the student has completed and how well they are engaged. They have a profile tab where they can change their password, email and account information. The final important part of the parents side of the app is the community tab. In this, the parent can connect with other guardians going through the same journey. This is also a connection for the parent to professionals and experts in the Autism and therapy domain.

In terms of developement, the app was initially prototyped in Proto.io and then coded in Android Studio using Java. However, the functionality of this app is so widespread that it is currently under further developement to improve that aspect.
